## Interview Question Template

### Opening Questions
1. **How did you get started with veganism?** (Origin story, relatability)
2. **What made you decide to share your vegan journey on social media?**
3. **What's the biggest misconception about vegan food in Minneapolis?**

### Minneapolis-Specific Questions
4. **What's your favorite vegan restaurant in Minneapolis right now?**
5. **Where do you do your grocery shopping? (Co-ops, specialty stores, etc.)**
6. **What Minneapolis vegan spot do you wish more people knew about?**
7. **How has the Minneapolis vegan scene changed since you started?**

### Practical Advice
8. **What's your go-to easy vegan meal when you're busy?**
9. **Best vegan product you've discovered recently?**
10. **Advice for someone going vegan in Minneapolis?**

### Community & Future
11. **What do you wish Minneapolis had more of for vegans?**
12. **What's next for you? Any upcoming projects or collaborations?**
13. **How can the Minneapolis vegan community support each other better?**

### Fun/Quick Fire Round
14. **Favorite Minneapolis vegan burger?**
15. **Best vegan dessert spot?**
16. **Go-to coffee shop with good vegan options?**
17. **One vegan ingredient you can't live without?**

## Outreach Strategy & Timeline

### Phase 1: Initial Outreach (Week 1-2)
- Research and verify social media handles
- Send personalized outreach to 5-8 influencers
- Prioritize those with highest engagement rates (not just follower counts)
- Send business owner emails to Herbivorous Butcher, Vegan East, J. Selby's

### Phase 2: Follow-Up (Week 2-3)
- Follow up with non-responders after 7 days (gentle nudge)
- Schedule interviews with those who respond positively
- Prepare customized question sets based on their content focus

### Phase 3: Interviews (Week 3-6)
- Conduct 2-3 interviews per week
- Option for written (email) or video (Zoom) format
- Keep it conversational and authentic

### Phase 4: Content Creation (Ongoing)
- Edit and format interviews
- Create graphics with influencer photos (get permission)
- Coordinate publish dates with interviewees

### Phase 5: Promotion (Post-Publish)
- Tag influencers when publishing their interview
- Create shareable graphics for Instagram/TikTok
- Email newsletter feature
- Cross-promote across all active interviews

## Interview Output Format

### Written Interview Structure:
1. **Intro paragraph** (Who they are, what they do, why they matter)
2. **Pull quote highlight** (Most interesting/inspiring quote)
3. **Q&A format** (Conversational, easy to scan)
4. **Quick Picks section** (Favorite spots, rapid-fire recommendations)
5. **Follow them** (Links to all their social profiles)
6. **Related content** (Links to restaurants/guides they mentioned)

### Example Intro:
> **Meet Sarah Chen: The Plant-Based Food Scientist Veganizing Minneapolis**
> 
> Sarah (@MinneapolisVegan) started her Instagram in 2022 as a way to document her transition to veganism. Three years later, she's one of the Twin Cities' most trusted voices for vegan restaurant reviews, with over 12k followers who rely on her honest takes and detailed food photography. We sat down with Sarah to talk about her favorite spots, the evolution of Minneapolis' vegan scene, and why the Herbivorous Butcher's Korean BBQ ribs changed her life.

## Backup Plan (If Response is Low)

**Alternative approaches:**
1. **Start with business owners** (easier to reach, more responsive)
2. **Offer incentives:** Free feature in newsletter, giveaway collaboration
3. **Partner with existing vegan events** (interviews at vegan festivals, markets)
4. **Create "Community Favorites" posts** instead (aggregate recommendations without direct interviews)
5. **Reach out to micro-influencers** (500-2k followers, often more engaged and responsive)

## Notes for Implementation

**Best practices:**
- Keep outreach genuine and personal (no copy-paste vibes)
- Respect people's time (async written interviews are easier for busy folks)
- Always give credit and tag properly
- Follow up, but don't pester
- Make it easy to say yes (provide questions upfront, flexible formats)

**Red flags to avoid:**
- Don't promise unrealistic reach/traffic
- Don't ask for free promotion without giving value
- Don't ghost after getting content
- Don't edit quotes without approval
